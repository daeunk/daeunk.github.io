Resume

a basic overview of my interests and happenings

Before 2013: Pre-College

pursuing violin

My main extracurricular before college was violin. Starting at the age of 5, I took private lessons, joined chamber music, and various youth orchestras around my area. My last pre-professional experience consisted of me being a pre-collegiate student at the San Francisco Conservatory of Music and the concertmistress of the San Francisco Symphony Youth Orchestra. I was also part of the Échappé Quartet, which won the Ensemble Prize in the Mondavi Center Young Arts Competition in 2012

2013-2017: Life as a Tree

college years

I went to Stanford University starting September 2013, studying Symbolic Systems, an interdisciplinary major encompassing computer science, philosophy, psychology, and linguistics. While there, I was also active in the Asian American Pacific Islander (AAPI) community, serving as an undergraduate representative for the Asian American Activities Center Advisory Board, the Asian American New Student Orientation Coordinator, Concert Committee Co-Chair for Listen to the Silence - the largest Asian American issues conference on the west coast - bringing artists Jhameel and DANakaDAN my term, as well as being a member of Sigma Psi Zeta. I graduated with my B.S. in June 2017

2015, 2016, 2017: Internships @ Google

pre-professional experience

Over 3 summers, I got the chance to intern at the Mountain View office (2015, 2017) and the NYC office (2016) of Google. Over the course of 3 summers, I've worked on Search, Jamboard, and Earth, and I've coded in JavaScript and C++

2016-2018: M.S. in Computer Science

prolonging my stay @ Stanford

I was accepted into the coterminal master's program in Computer Science at Stanford during my junior year. I took the extra 2 quarters past my bachelor's to expand my technical skills and hone in on my Human-Computer Interaction track with UX design classes. I graduated with my M.S. in March 2018

2018: Filmmaking Venture

a new hobby

I discovered a new passion in filmmaking, specifically to tell underrepresented stories. Based on my own experience with a foreign name, I entered the AT&T Shape Create-a-Thon challenge in June 2018, where the video eventually placed in the Top 4 out of 500+ script submissions and 60 finalist videos

2018: Internship @ Pixar Animation Studios

melding film and tech

Before I start my full time job, I took the opportunity to join Pixar Animation Studios as a Post Production Engineering Intern. Mainly coding in Python and JavaScript, I helped create and improve tools for the post production folks through migrating Qt apps, implementing caching, and testing the pipeline for Toy Story remastering. I was also able to bring in my design thinking knowledge to lead a design sprint with my team to help set long term product goals

What am I up to now?

In September 2018, I will be joining Google NYC as a Software Engineer under Docs and Slides, helping users create beautiful and rich content. In addition, I now run my own YouTube channel DNKM Stories on the side, in the pursuit of telling further stories around the Asian American community.
