about.

My name is Da Eun Kim, and I'm a software engineer at Google LA, working in Ads Planning, and before that, I was a software engineer at Google NYC, working on Slides. I'm also a recent graduate from Stanford University (M.S. Computer Science, B.S. Symbolic Systems).
I strongly believe that technology is best used as a tool and means, rather than the solution and goal. With a great user experience in mind and the technical skills, I hope to be at the service of the communities around me. My long term interests lie somewhere between civic tech and entertainment.
I'm a filmmaker (YouTube channel) dedicated to creating visibility for underrepresented stories about and within the Asian American community. I was a finalist in the 2018 AT&T Shape Create-a-thon Competition (in collaboration with Warner Bros.) and a fellow in the 2019 Jubilee Fellowship.
I'm a podcast producer and host for bamboo & glass, a space to highlight stories of Asian American womxn that anyone can learn from. My co-host and I explore topics such as being womxn in tech, traveling with family, our college application processes, how we learned about politics, and what it can mean to be an ethical sellout. You can listen to our podcast on Spotify, Apple Podcasts, Google Podcasts, and more!
